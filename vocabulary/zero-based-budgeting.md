# Zero-Based Budgeting

## Definition

The core philosophy: every cent of income is given a job and allocated to something. The goal is income minus total allocations equals zero. Allocations are ephemeral per budget period — they don't carry forward.

## Context

**Theory vs practice.** Conceptually, every cent is allocated. In practice, the system intentionally doesn't allocate everything — the unallocated remainder is discretionary spending, split equally between husband and wife with no questions asked. There's no official tracking of this discretionary spending, though you could figure it out with effort. The system doesn't provide that effort.

This is zero-based in spirit with a deliberate gap. The gap is a feature, not a bug — it gives breathing room and avoids micromanaging personal spending.

## Contract

- Total income minus total allocation = discretionary (untracked).
- Allocations exist only within their budget period.
- The system does not enforce that all income is allocated.
